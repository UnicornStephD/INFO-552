Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to some common concerns about attending Al-Anon.

Do I have to talk at my first meeting?

Nope. You are welcome to just listen. Many people attend several meetings before they feel comfortable sharing, and that’s completely okay.

Is Al-Anon only for spouses of alcoholics?

Not at all. Al-Anon is for anyone affected by someone else’s drinking — including parents, siblings, adult children, friends, and more.

Do I have to believe in God to attend?

No. Al-Anon is a spiritual, not religious, program. You are encouraged to define a “Higher Power” in your own way — or not at all. People of all (or no) faith backgrounds are welcome.

Will I be judged for talking about my situation?

No. Al-Anon meetings are confidential and nonjudgmental. Everyone is there to focus on their own recovery — not to critique yours.

How do I find the right meeting for me?

There are many types of meetings — in person, online, phone-based. It’s okay to try different ones until you find a group that feels like a good fit.
