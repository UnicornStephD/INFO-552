What to Expect at a Meeting

It’s normal to feel uncertain before your first meeting — here’s what you can expect.

Al-Anon meetings are welcoming, safe, and respectful spaces. You’ll find a group of people who share a common bond: the impact of someone else’s drinking on their lives.

Here’s what typically happens:

  • You’ll be greeted, but no one will pressure you to talk.
  • Meetings often begin with brief readings that outline the program’s principles.
  • Members share their experiences, strength, and hope — there’s no cross-talk, fixing, or advice-giving.
  • You can simply listen, or share when you feel comfortable. Sharing is always optional.
  • Most meetings last about an hour, and you’re welcome to leave at any time.
  • There’s no fee to attend, though a small donation may be collected toward meeting expenses — giving is optional.

You don’t need to prepare or bring anything. Just showing up is enough.
