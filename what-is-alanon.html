What is Al-Anon?

A community of mutual support for people affected by someone else’s drinking.

Al-Anon is a worldwide fellowship that offers a safe, anonymous space for individuals who are impacted by the drinking behavior of a loved one — whether it's a partner, parent, sibling, friend, or anyone else in their life.

It’s a program built on mutual support, shared experiences, and gentle guidance — not advice-giving or therapy. Members attend meetings to listen, learn, and connect with others who understand the stress, confusion, and pain of loving someone who drinks excessively.

Al-Anon is not affiliated with any religion or political group, and it’s not professional counseling. Instead, it’s a spiritual program grounded in shared tools and values, offering long-term support through principles like acceptance, detachment with love, and personal growth.

You don’t have to identify as being in crisis to attend. Many people join out of quiet concern, long-term frustration, or curiosity about healing — and that’s enough.
