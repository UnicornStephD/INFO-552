Is Al-Anon Right for Me?

Al-Anon welcomes anyone whose life has been affected by someone else's drinking — in any way, at any stage.

If you’ve ever felt:

  • Confused about how to help someone who drinks too much
  • Frustrated by broken promises, mood swings, or chaos caused by drinking
  • Exhausted from trying to “hold it all together”
  • Worried, anxious, or resentful — even when things seem “fine” on the surface
  • Like you’re always waiting for the next crisis or letdown
  • Alone in your experience or ashamed to talk about it

— then you are not alone. Many people in Al-Anon started with these same feelings.

You don’t have to label anyone or define their behavior. Al-Anon isn't about diagnosing others — it’s about finding your own peace and clarity, regardless of what someone else chooses to do.

There’s no cost to attend a meeting. You can listen quietly, share if you wish, and explore whether the support feels helpful. Many people attend a few meetings before they feel ready to engage — and that’s perfectly okay.
