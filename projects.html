Projects from Other Courses

  • Rethinking Archival Practices: Decolonization, Ethics, and Community Engagement — INFO 560 (PDF)
    A literature review exploring ethical appraisal, inclusive representation, and community-based archival strategies within the decolonization movement.
  • Fedora: Digital Stewardship Systems Review and Analysis — INFO 591 (PDF)
    A comprehensive evaluation of Fedora as a flexible, open-source digital repository for long-term preservation, with special focus on its use by smaller institutions. The project explored metadata standards, version control, and scalability within the OAIS framework.
  • The Ethos of Private Libraries: And their Contribution to an Ethically Conscious Society — INFO 505 (PDF)
    A paper exploring the ethical role of private libraries in promoting equitable access, preserving cultural knowledge, and fostering intellectual freedom through community engagement and historical stewardship.

Co-Authored Publications & Research

  • Association of Coronary Artery Calcification and Mortality in the National Lung Screening Trial: A Comparison of Three Scoring Methods
    Co-author of a clinical research paper published in Radiology, highlighting that simple visual assessment of coronary artery calcification (CAC) on low-dose CT scans used for lung cancer screening is strongly associated with coronary heart disease and all-cause mortality, performing comparably to more complex CAC scoring methods.
  • CRISPR-engineered T cells in patients with refractory cancer
    Co-author of a clinical research paper published in Science, this first-in-human trial demonstrates that CRISPR-Cas9–engineered T cells targeting multiple genes can be safely administered to patients with advanced cancer, showing durable engraftment, tumor targeting, and feasibility for future immunotherapy development.
